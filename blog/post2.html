Gen Z and Brand Communications: Lessons from NSAC

March 15, 2025 • 7 min read

Featured Image Placeholder

As the Strategy Director for our National Student Advertising Competition (NSAC) team working with AT&T, I've had the unique opportunity to deeply explore how brands can effectively connect with Gen Z. This experience has provided valuable insights that extend beyond the competition and offer broader lessons for brand communications in today's rapidly evolving landscape.

Understanding the Digital Dreamer

Our research for AT&T revealed a core segment of Gen Z we termed "Digital Dreamers" – young adults transitioning to independence who increasingly value their online communities and make financial decisions that balance responsibility with flexibility. This insight proved crucial in developing a campaign that resonated with this audience on an emotional level.

Digital Dreamers are redefining independence on their own terms. They crave autonomy and the freedom to choose their path, but not at the cost of connection. For them, being independent doesn't mean being alone – a nuance that many brands miss in their communications.

The Power of Authentic Recognition

One of the most powerful lessons from our work with AT&T was the importance of authentic recognition. Our campaign, "Small Wins, Big Moves," celebrated Digital Dreamers for the incremental steps they take toward independence – whether covering their part of the phone bill or carving out their identity online.

This approach resonated because it acknowledged the reality of Gen Z's journey: progress isn't always linear, and traditional milestones are being reimagined. Brands that recognize and validate these new definitions of success can forge stronger connections with this audience.

Beyond Price: Value-Added Relationships

While pricing is important to Gen Z, our research showed they use partnerships and benefits to differentiate between brands in a commoditized market. Gen Z wants to know how a brand can be a value-add to their lives – not just a service provider.

This insight informed our strategy to position AT&T as more than just a telecommunications company but as a partner in Gen Z's journey toward independence and connection. By emphasizing how AT&T enables meaningful connections rather than just providing a service, we created a more compelling value proposition.

Digital-First, But Human-Centered

Another key lesson from NSAC was the importance of digital-first, but human-centered communications. Gen Z is the first truly digital-native generation, but they still crave authentic human connection. Our campaign leveraged digital channels but focused on telling human stories that evoked emotion and fostered a sense of belonging.

This approach was particularly evident in our social media strategy, which emphasized user-generated content and real stories from Digital Dreamers. By amplifying authentic voices rather than pushing traditional brand messaging, we created a more engaging and relatable campaign.

Applying These Insights Beyond NSAC

The lessons learned from NSAC have broad applications for brands seeking to connect with Gen Z:

  • Focus on emotional relevance over functional benefits
  • Recognize and celebrate non-traditional paths and milestones
  • Create value beyond your core product or service
  • Balance digital innovation with human authenticity
  • Enable connection rather than simply facilitating transactions

Conclusion

Working on the AT&T campaign for NSAC has reinforced my belief in the power of strategic, insight-driven communications. By truly understanding the values, aspirations, and behaviors of Gen Z, brands can move beyond surface-level engagement to create meaningful connections that drive both business results and positive social impact.

As I continue my journey in communications strategy and marketing analytics, I'm excited to apply these lessons to help brands navigate the evolving landscape of consumer engagement and build lasting relationships with their audiences.
