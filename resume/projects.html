The National Student Advertising Competition (NSAC) is a prestigious competition where college students create comprehensive advertising campaigns for real clients. Here's my contribution to our team's campaign:

Campaign: Small Wins, Big Moves

Our team developed a strategic advertising campaign for AT&T targeting Gen Z, focusing on building an emotional connection with "Digital Dreamers" - young adults advancing to independence while still valuing their online communities.

My Role

  • Conducted market research on Gen Z attitudes toward telecom providers
  • Analyzed data to identify the target persona of "Digital Dreamers"
  • Contributed to media strategy and placement recommendations
  • Developed KPIs to measure campaign success

Campaign Highlights

  • Targeted strategy to increase brand advocacy from NPS 20 to 33
  • Projected to generate over 5 billion impressions
  • Created experiential marketing activations across multiple cities
  • Integrated campaign across streaming, social, broadcast, and experiential channels

Kay Bailey Hutchison Energy Finance Case Competition

Team Member

2025

The Kay Bailey Hutchison Energy Finance Case Competition challenged teams to develop comprehensive investment recommendations for potential acquisitions in the energy sector.

Project: Acquisition of Powell Industries, Inc.

Our team, "Ethical Energy Investing," created a detailed investment memo proposing the acquisition of Powell Industries, Inc. (NASDAQ:POWL), a global electric equipment manufacturing company.

My Role

  • Analyzed market trends in the electrical equipment manufacturing industry
  • Contributed to the development of operational efficiency strategies
  • Helped identify high-growth market opportunities for post-acquisition expansion
  • Assisted in creating sensitivity analyses for different growth scenarios

Key Investment Highlights

  • Deal Size: $2,116 million with targeted IRR of 33.2% and MOIC of 4.2x
  • Identified opportunities in data center infrastructure, renewable energy, and smart grid technology
  • Developed post-acquisition value creation strategy focused on operational efficiencies, market expansion, and product innovation
  • Proposed exit strategy via public listing or strategic sale in 5 years (2029)
